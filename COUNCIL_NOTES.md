## v1.2 ŌĆö "Enforce the craft rules we already preach"

**Tagline**: Turn the four most-cited writing rules from passive aspirations into hard, self-audited gates ŌĆö all inside `lib/prompts.ts` with zero schema or signature changes.

### 1. AND/BUT/THEREFORE self-revision gate (~20 min, must-have)

**PM**: Narrative  
**File**: `lib/prompts.ts` only

Append a mandatory SILENT final-pass to the END of the `SHARED_RULES` template literal (just before its closing backtick):

> "BEFORE you emit JSON, silently re-read your line sequence. For each adjacent pair of beats, name the connector: is it BUT (a complication), THEREFORE (a consequence), or AND-THEN (mere sequence)? Every AND-THEN is a defect ŌĆö rewrite the second beat so it either complicates or pays off the first. Do not output the audit; output only the repaired script."

Why at the END: a rule the model reads once at the top gets deprioritized by line 40. A silent self-audit at generation time is how you actually catch it.

### 2. The Promise Line + plant-the-callback (~25 min, must-have)

**PM**: Director + Comedy (convergence)  
**File**: `lib/prompts.ts` only

Two adjacent bullets added to the COLD OPEN block in `SHARED_RULES`:

**(A) THE PROMISE** ŌĆö within the first ~20s, after the opening image, the lead plants ONE real, specific unanswered question from the source (a stake, not a table-of-contents). Its own forbidden-teaser list: "and that raises a bigger question," "stick around," "buckle up," "you'll want to hear this."

**(B) RUNNERS & CALLBACKS** ŌĆö plant ONE vivid real detail (exact phrase, absurd real noun, number) early, name it plainly once, then reuse that EXACT phrase ONCE near the close, recontextualized. Never flag the callback ("remember when we saidŌĆ”") ŌĆö just reuse and trust.

Both must be REAL source details ŌĆö if the source is mundane, the promise is a quieter real stake, never manufactured.

### 3. Verbatim-quote vs paraphrase rule (~20 min, must-have)

**PM**: Journalism  
**File**: `lib/prompts.ts` only

The prompts say "attribution before quotes" and "never invent facts" but nothing forbids inventing the WORDS inside quotation marks.

Replace the single attribution bullet in SHARED_RULES with two:

- **VERBATIM-QUOTE**: the word "quote" may only precede words that literally appear in the SOURCE, attributed to the real speaker who said them. If you can't find the exact words, summarize in the host's own voice WITHOUT "quote."
- **RESEARCH-IS-NEVER-A-QUOTE**: research findings must be paraphrased and hedged ("reporting in this space suggestsŌĆ”"), never attributed as a named participant's exact words.

Mirror as MASTER_RULES item 6 (insert before the BANNED PHRASES section) for override priority.

### 4. Audio tags: pause-as-punctuation, front-loaded emotion, one-per-line (~20 min, should-have)

**PM**: Audio  
**File**: `lib/prompts.ts` only

Edit the AUDIO TAG VOCABULARY block in `SHARED_RULES` to add three bullets:
1. Emotion/pace tags go at the FRONT of the line they modify ŌĆö a trailing `[sarcastic]` colors nothing in v3.
2. `[pauses]`/`[long pause]` is the STRONGEST tool ŌĆö place it immediately BEFORE a reveal/name/number (lean-in), never AFTER a punchline (the silence after a joke is the laugh's job).
3. Hard cap: ONE tag per line max (two tags on one isolated TTS call fight each other).

Keep the existing allowlist and `[eye-roll]`/`[chef's kiss]` ban verbatim.
